The 2017 paper "Attention Is All You Need" didn't just introduce a new architecture — it redefined what was possible in AI.

## The Problem Before Transformers

RNNs and LSTMs processed sequences step by step. This meant:

- **Slow training** — you couldn't parallelize sequential processing
- **Vanishing gradients** — long-range dependencies got lost
- **Bottleneck** — the entire input had to compress into a fixed-size vector

## What Self-Attention Changed

Self-attention lets every token in a sequence attend to every other token *simultaneously*. Instead of reading a sentence word by word, the model sees everything at once and decides what matters.

```
Attention(Q, K, V) = softmax(QK^T / √d_k) V
```

Three simple matrix multiplications. That's it. No recurrence, no convolutions.

## Why It Worked

1. **Parallelization** — every position computes independently, so you can throw GPUs at it
2. **Long-range dependencies** — token 1 can directly attend to token 1000
3. **Scalability** — more data + more compute = better performance (scaling laws)

## The Ripple Effect

Transformers gave us GPT, BERT, and every large language model since. They've expanded beyond NLP into vision (ViT), audio, protein folding, and more.

The lesson: sometimes the right *simplification* is more powerful than the right *complication*.
